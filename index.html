School Team Qualifies For Finals

By Zaid Takriti | December 4, 2025

The Pacific Coast Academy school soccer team celebrating after qualifying for the finals.

Calgary, Alberta — The Pacific Coast Academy school soccer team made history by defeating Redwood Grove’s rivals 2-1 at Redwood Grove Field on Tuesday, December 2, to secure a spot in the state finals.

In a close and intense match, Pacific Coast Academy took control early with a goal from captain Zizoo Takriti in the first half. Midfielder Rico Aiyal created several chances and assisted Zizoo’s first goal, helping the team prevent the opponents from scoring. In the second half, striker Nabil scored the winning goal, sealing the 2‑1 victory and showing the team’s passion, teamwork, and smart play under pressure.

Coach Abdul Rafi praised the team’s effort, saying, “The players stayed focused and worked together throughout the game. Scoring the second goal showed their hard work and smart play we planned for all season. This win is the result of dedication, teamwork, and determination that we do on and off the pitch.”

The stands at Redwood Grove Field were packed with cheering fans, their excitement echoing across the field after every play. The Pacific Coast fan Mousi said, “The team played so well! Watching Zizoo and Rico work together was amazing. That winning goal in the second half got everyone on their feet. I can’t wait to see them in the finals and hopefully we can get to see them lift the trophy.

The two team captains focus on the championship trophy, determined to bring it home in the upcoming final.

With the win, Pacific Coast Academy now looks forward to the state finals against ISNA Giants on December 8, 2025, aiming to claim the championship for the first time in the school's history. The team’s skill, teamwork, and hard work have excited students, teachers, and fans, creating a buzzing and electric atmosphere that promises the finals will be even more thrilling. Pacific Coast Academy will now compete in the state finals, representing the school in the championship for the first time.
